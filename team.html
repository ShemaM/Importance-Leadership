Words of Team Leader

Our CEO

Fiston Ndayishimiye, the founder of Importance Leadership, is on a mission to transform the lives of young people into leaders and empower them to become agents of change. Born in the Democratic Republic of Congo, Fiston's journey exemplifies the resilience and determination of his people, the Banyamulenge, who have long been viewed with suspicion despite their deep roots in Congolese society.

As Fiston reflects on his path, he is reminded of the words of the great Patrice Lumumba, the first democratically elected Prime Minister of the Congo, who once declared, "We are no longer your monkeys." Lumumba's unwavering commitment to the independence and unity of the Congo, despite the efforts of Western powers to undermine him, continues to inspire Fiston in his struggle for self-determination. Echoing Martin Luther King Jr.'s sentiments, Fiston believes that "Darkness cannot drive out darkness; only light can do that." Through his work with Importance Leadership, he seeks to be that light, guiding the youth toward a brighter future. "There are millions of untapped people who are waiting to hear my voice and receive words of knowledge, empowerment, encouragement, inspiration, and motivation," Fiston declares, channeling the spirit of Muhammad Ali, who famously said, "Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee. The hands can't hit what the eyes can't see." Fiston's journey has not been without its challenges. Like Malcolm X, who once stated, "The only way we'll get freedom for ourselves is to identify ourselves with every oppressed person in the world," Fiston has had to navigate the complexities of his identity as an American African, deeply connected to his African roots, but also shaped by the realities of life in the United States. "I am American on papers but African in heart and blood," Fiston explains, a sentiment that resonates with the experiences of countless other immigrants and first-generation Americans who strive to bridge the divide between their ancestral heritage and their adopted home. Through his work, Fiston aims to empower the next generation of leaders, echoing the words of Lumumba, who declared, "The Congo's independence is a decisive step towards the liberation of the entire African continent." By inspiring young people to become "agents of change," Fiston hopes to ignite a spark that will illuminate the path toward a more just and equitable future for all.

Our Able Team

Bonkey's remarkable journey has taken him from the struggles of being a refugee in Burundi to becoming a dedicated humanitarian and development professional.

Born in the DRC, Bonkey's childhood was abruptly disrupted by conflicts and wars, forcing his family to flee their home and seek refuge in Bujumbura, Burundi, where they lived for over eight years. In 2015, a new wave of unrest broke out in Burundi due to political tensions, compelling Bonkey and his family to flee once again, leading them to seek safety in Kenya. Life as a refugee has been fraught with countless challenges, including discrimination, poverty, unemployment, inequality, lack of representation, and limited access to education. Bonkey vividly remembers the struggle to secure even one meal a day and the immense difficulties in acquiring formal education.
